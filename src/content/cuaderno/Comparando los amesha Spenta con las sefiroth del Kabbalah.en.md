The Amesha Spenta of Zoroastrianism and the Sefirot of Kabbalah share a strikingly similar structure: both are divine emanations that mediate between the absolute and the material.

![](/images/cuaderno/captura.jpg)

On the left side we see the sefirotic tree with Keter at the top and Malkuth at the base. On the right side, the Amesha Spenta with Ahura Mazda at the summit. The correspondence is not one-to-one, but the pattern is undeniable: six pairs of opposites balanced along a central axis.

**Some notable correspondences:**

- Keter (Crown) ≈ Vohu Manah (Good Mind) — both represent the first emanation, divine will
- Chokhmah/Wisdom and Binah/Understanding ≈ Asha Vahishta (Ultimate Truth) — binary structure of wisdom
- Tiferet (Beauty) ≈ Spenta Armaiti (Devotion) — central balance point
- Malkuth (Kingdom) ≈ Ameretat (Immortality) — manifestation in the physical world

Is this a case of cultural diffusion or universal archetypes? Probably both. The Zoroastrian magi and medieval Kabbalists shared the same impulse: mapping the invisible.
