Preliminary study for a series of illustrations about the Mesopotamian pantheon. Sin, the moon god, depicted as a white bull facing Humbaba, the guardian of the Cedar Forest.

The composition plays with the idea of reflection: both characters share features, as if the beast were the dark mirror of the god. The palette will be limited: white, black, and a single lapis lazuli accent.

**Technical notes:**
- Graphite on Fabriano paper, 200 gsm
- Dimensions: 29.7 × 42 cm (A3)
- Estimated time: 12 hours

The final result will go in the gallery once finished.
